Introduction

Clash Royale has just turned 10 years old, bringing friends together over a fun yet strategic online match where each player has a deck of 8 cards, each serving a different role with the end goal being to take as many of their opponents towers within 5 minutes without losing their own. Considering there are now 143 cards in total, there is an innumerable amount of combinations that can be used to win. These combinations led to some players rising to the top of the ladder, while other fall and never progress. But the question arises: are call cards balanced? The short answer is no, absolutely not. But if thats the case, which card is the most broken/annoying to deal with in a match?

What constitutes whether card is annoying? Here's a simple formula to decide how balanced a card is

cardbalanced in encounters + meta changessufficient? = balanced if a card is not balanced in its regular encounters with a card, and the meta changes hasn't changed its key stats enough to make it normal, then it is not balanced, and therefore annoying to come across

    How did these cards earn their place here?

  • 1. The Boss Bandit: everyone knows the name, and no one likes to hear it. Added to the game in April 2025, she was a 6 elixir tank and one of the 8 champions in the game, and like all champions, she had a special ability. This ability allowed her to dash backwards a few tiles and make herself invisbile to enemies for 1 second, then if she can lock on, dash again towards that target to deal extra damage. And this ability? Only costs 1 elixir (and it can be used twice in one go). The reason she is annoying is because she is nearly impossible to kill with her dashing around all the time, resetting tank killers like the inferno tower, and the only real way to take her out is with swarm cards, which a lot of people don't use in their decks now.
  • 2. The Evolution Megaknight: as if the normal Megaknight wasn't bad enough, Supercell decided to release the evolution of him to make matches more unfair. For context, the normal Megaknight was a 7 elixir tank that dealt damage when deployed (no other card can do that), dealt a fair amount of splash damage, and dealt a significant amount of damage when he jumped! His initial purpose was to act as a defense 'panic button', like when your opponent has a large push coming to your tower and you don't have the right cards on hand to deal with it, you just put the Megaknight down on the whole thing and watch the push disintegrate, and the only way to defend him was with a mini tank in the centre. The evolution, in this case, now made him more insufferable since it lets him punch things backwards into the air, making defending him nearly impossible and making ranged troops like the dart goblin more of a likely substitute into your deck
  • 3. The Prince: The Prince was one of the first few cards added to the game when it was released in 2016, and was the most popular choice when unlocking epic cards. Having been in the game for so long, you would think the dev team would be able to balance him, right? Wrong! He has consistently been unbalanced in every patch update, and its annoying players (inluding me) to the hilt. Essentially, he is a 5 elixir 'mini tank' purely designed to deal high damage, mostly on offense. After he travels at normal speed for 2.5 tiles, he goes into a full blown charge moving as fast as 2 tiles per second and dealing double the regular melee damage. The problem, however, doesn't lie in his high damage or insane charge speed; it lies in his absurd health. Nearly 2000 hitpoints for a card dealing nearly Mini P.E.K.K.A level damage is absolutely not fair, even if it's fairly expensive
  • 4. The Evolution Witch: the witch has been part of the Clash Universe for as long as the game has been alive, and generally her reputation has been quite solid. She's a 5 elixir medium-health ranged support troop with the special ability of spawning 4 skeletons every 7 seconds, making her quite the weapon when both defending multiple troops as well as when building strong pushes. However, when her evolution arrived in May 5 of 2025, she completely broke the game; her evolution allowed her to heal a small bit every time one of her skeletons died, meaning she not only healed herself after going against troops that should have killed her, she also increases her maximum level of health when opponents use spells like poison, which nowadays make her near unkillable. Due to this broken healing mechanic, she is now in every midladder-menace deck there is, destroying clumped pushes and taking down towers with her constant stream of skeletons distracting your defense
  • 5. The Hero Mini P.E.K.K.A: Mini P.E.K.K.A was also one of the first cards introduced to the game back in 2016, and even given its stats, its a relatively balanced card. For 4 elixir, you get a tank-killer with a moderate amount of health that also runs fairly quickly. It can destroy beatdown decks and can be threatening in a counterpush, but it also gets easily countered by the 1 elixir skeletons (as an example), so really its a balanced card. However, as one of the 4 chosen cards to become the first heroes released back in December 2025, it was soon bound to change. When it first released as a hero, the ability of Mini P.E.K.K.A allowed him to increase his level based on how far a bar above his head had filled up to. Let's say the hero mini P.E.K.K.A managed to kill off a golem along with some goblins, and he was left at half health. For 1 elixir, he could heal himself by 50% (so back to full health) AND increase his level by 6 (let's say he's level 11, in this encounter he would then become level 17), which meant all defending troops would be demolished and he would wipe out the tower in 4 hits. This card paired up with a Royal Chef tower (which increases levels of troops by 1 every so often) was every players worst nightmare.
  • 6. The Evolution Lumberjack: Lumberjack is a very unusal card in Clash Royale, and no other card is like him at all. He's a 4 elixir support troop with moderate health (about 1200), as well as a lot of damage (281 per hit, so about 300 per second), so nothing too weird as of now; But when he dies, he drops a rage spell, boosting friendly troops in movement and attack speed, making him incredibly difficult to defend. Due to this unique ability, players over the years paired him up with slow but deadly cards to make them an even bigger threat, the most popular synergy being balloon (most people will be able to tell this is what sparked the infamous 'Lumberloon' deck). Then, Supercell decided he wasn't broken enough, and gave him an evolution. With this evolution, once the Lumberjack dies and drops a rage spell, he spawns his own ghost that, as long as the rage spell is running, it will attack anything in front of it with the same amount fo damage and hitspeed. Anything. And when i say as long the rage spell is running, that means that if you use the evo, then drop the rage spell before it stops, then mirror it again, the ghost keeps attacking until the tower is practically gone. Talk about relentless
  • 7. The Electro Giant: Released in 2018 as part of a large update adding various new cards like Sparky, Goblin Giant anf Goblin Drill, the Electro Giant was a new concept as a beatdown win condition, and at the time, it was the most brutal to deal with. For 7 elixir, you get a regular giant with only 200 less health than the original, but with a batter pack on his back that zaps, damages and resets anything in its radius. So if you were to use a skeleton army to take it down like you would with any other giant, they would all disappear because of the zap ability. If you instead were to use a tank-killer like the inferno dragon, that would just get reset while in the EG's radius and become useless. It was broken because there were very limited answers to take it down fully, most of which people don't use (like goblin cage), and even then these solutions didn't always work, as players using EG paired him up with a lightning spell to evaporate any defense troops you would use to snipe it down
  • 8: The Hero Musketeer: Another one of the 4 cards selected to be the first 4 heroes, this card was deemed to be possibly the most broken hero on release. For 3 elixir, the musketeer spawned a turret with as much health as a miner (1300 roughly), hit speed and range of a dart goblin (0.5s, 6.5 tiles) and the damage of the musketeer herself (>200).
