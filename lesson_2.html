Clash Royale

since its 10 years old now, something needs to be discussed...

Clash Royale has just turned 10 years old, bringing friends together over a fun yet strategic online match where each player has a deck of 8 cards, each serving a different role with the end goal being to take as many of their opponents towers within 5 minutes without losing their own. Considering there are now 143 cards in total, there is an innumerable amount of combinations that can be used to win. These combinations led to some players rising to the top of the ladder, while other fall and never progress. But the question arises: are call cards balanced? The short answer is no, absolutely not. But if thats the case, which card is the most broken/annoying to deal with in a match?

What constitutes whether card is annoying? Here's a simple formula to decide how balanced a card is

cardbalanced in encounters + meta changessufficient? = balanced if a card is not balanced in its regular encounters with a card, and the meta changes hasn't changed its key stats enough to make it normal, then it is not balanced, and therefore annoying to come across

Here is a list of the top 10 most annoying cards in Clash Royale history (including evos and heroes):

  • 1. Boss Bandit
  • 2. Evolution Megaknight
  • 3. Prince
  • 4. Evolution Witch
  • 5. Hero Mini P.E.K.K.A
  • 6. Evolution Lumberjack
  • 7. Firecracker
  • 8. Hero Musketeer
  • 9. Evolution Skeleton Army
  • 10. X-Bow

    • How did these cards earn their place here?

  • 1. The Boss Bandit: everyone knows the name, and no one likes to hear it. Added to the game in April 2025, she was a 6 elixir tank and one of the 8 champions in the game, and like all champions, she had a special ability. This ability allowed her to dash backwards a few tiles and make herself invisbile to enemies for 1 second, then if she can lock on, dash again towards that target to deal extra damage. And this ability? Only costs 1 elixir (and it can be used twice in one go). The reason she is annoying is because she is nearly impossible to kill with her dashing around all the time, resetting tank killers like the inferno tower, and the only real way to take her out is with swarm cards, which a lot of people don't use in their decks now.
  • 2. The Evolution Megaknight: as if the normal Megaknight wasn't bad enough, Supercell decided to release the evolution of him to make matches more unfair. For context, the normal Megaknight was a 7 elixir tank that dealt damage when deployed (no other card can do that), dealt a fair amount of splash damage, and dealt a significant amount of damage when he jumped! His initial purpose was to act as a defense 'panic button', like when your opponent has a large push coming to your tower and you don't have the right cards on hand to deal with it, you just put the Megaknight down on the whole thing and watch the push disintegrate, and the only way to defend him was with a mini tank in the centre. The evolution, in this case, now made him more insufferable since it lets him punch things backwards into the air, making defending him nearly impossible and making ranged troops like the dart goblin more of a likely substitute into your deck
  • 3. The Prince: The Prince was one of the first few cards added to the game when it was released in 2016, and was the most popular choice when unlocking epic cards. Having been in the game for so long, you would think the dev team would be able to balance him, right? Wrong! He has consistently been unbalanced in every patch update, and its annoying players (inluding me) to the hilt. Essentially, he is a 5 elixir 'mini tank' purely designed to deal high damage, mostly on offense. After he travels at normal speed for 2.5 tiles, he goes into a full blown charge moving as fast as 2 tiles per second and dealing double the regular melee damage. The problem, however, doesn't lie in his high damage or insane charge speed; it lies in his absurd health. Nearly 2000 hitpoints for a card dealing nearly Mini P.E.K.K.A level is absolutely not fair, even if it's expensive damage
